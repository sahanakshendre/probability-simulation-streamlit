The simulation converges to the true probability because of a mathematical principle called the Law of Large Numbers.

In probability experiments like coin toss or dice roll, each outcome is random. In a small number of trials, results may vary significantly due to randomness. However, as the number of trials increases, the randomness averages out.

This means that the relative frequency of an outcome (experimental probability) becomes closer to its actual probability (theoretical probability).

For example, in a fair coin toss:

Theoretical probability of heads = 0.5
In 10 trials → may get 7 heads (0.7)
In 1000 trials → will be close to 0.5

Thus, increasing the number of simulations leads to more accurate probability estimation.
